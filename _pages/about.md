I am a PhD student in Civil Engineering at the [University of Houston](https://www.uh.edu/), advised by [Dr. Abigail L. Beck](https://www.cee.uh.edu/faculty/beck-abby). My research focuses on **infrastructure and community resilience**, with particular interest in:

- Probabilistic fragility and resilience assessment of infrastructure systems
- Risk-informed decision support for infrastructure network safety and operation
- Restoration and recovery modeling at the infrastructure and community level
- Infrastructure asset management and lifecycle planning
- Digital twins and data-driven condition monitoring of infrastructure systems

Currently, I work with the [Beck Research Group](https://beck.cive.uh.edu/) on probabilistic risk assessment of electrical power distribution networks in Houston and Galveston under wind hazards, developing fragility models to characterize vulnerability at the component and network levels.

Before starting my PhD, I completed my M.Sc. in Digital Engineering at [Bauhaus-Universität Weimar](https://www.uni-weimar.de/en/university/start/), Germany, where I developed a Hybrid Variational AutoEncoder and Covolutional Neural Network framework for structural damage and sensor fault prognosis in prestressed concrete bridges. I also have four years of experience working as a structural engineer in the UK and Nigeria, analyzing and designing telecom structures, residential buildings, and multistory reinforced concrete and steel structures.
